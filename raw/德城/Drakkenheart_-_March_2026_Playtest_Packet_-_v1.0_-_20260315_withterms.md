# **Gold in Drakkenheim**

Dungeons of Drakkenheim uses its own scale for wealth instead of the scale in the 【《匕首之心》 (Daggerheart)】 core rulebook.

In this 【战役 (campaign)】 guide, use the following denominations and conversions:

- 10 handfuls of gold = 1 【袋 (bag)】 of gold
- 10 bags of gold = 1 sack of gold
- 10 sacks of gold = 1 【箱 (chest)】 of gold

# **Contaminated 【领域 (Domain)】 Cards**

Drakkenheim is host to many strange and 【*惊怖 Terrifying - 被动：* 当【敌人名称】成功攻击时，所有近距离范围内的玩家角色失去 1 希望点，而你获得 1 恐惧点。 (terrifying)】 powers – unheard of magics, warped weaves of reality, and more. PCs who engage with the city and its dangers can gain access to Contaminated 【领域 (domain)】 cards, powerful variants of the 【标准 (standard)】 【领域 (domain)】 cards.

Every 【领域 (domain)】 has several contaminated 【领域 (domain)】 cards, each a variation on a 【标准 (standard)】 【领域卡 (domain card)】. When you gain a contaminated 【领域卡 (domain card)】, you can add it to your 【配置 (loadout)】 or your 【宝库 (Vault)】. From then on, it operates as any other 【领域卡 (domain card)】, with the following exception: You cannot have both a contaminated and un-contaminated version of a 【领域卡 (domain card)】 in your 【配置 (loadout)】 at the same time. One of the two must always be in your 【宝库 (Vault)】, though you can recall one and 【宝库 (vault)】 the other 【当你这样做时 (when you do)】 so.

Here are a few core options for how you might gain access to Contaminated 【领域 (Domain)】 cards, but you should decide what approach makes sense for your game. Gaining contaminated 【领域 (domain)】 cards should not be easy, but players that seek them should be able to acquire them, given enough time and effort.

## **Surviving Contamination**

Contamination can be purged, but something happens to a soul and mind when it comes close to the edge. Delerium's power leaves a mark on you, unlocking new avenues for power.

When you 【长臂 (reach)】 contamination level 3 or higher and then purge your contamination entirely, you can gain access to a contaminated 【领域卡 (domain card)】 from one of your domains, up to 【位阶 (tier)】 3.

When you 【长臂 (reach)】 contamination level 5 and then purge your contamination entirely, you can gain access to a contaminated 【领域卡 (domain card)】 up to 【位阶 (tier)】 4. You cannot learn a contaminated 【领域卡 (domain card)】 above your level, but once you have survived contamination level 5 and come back from the brink, you can gain a 【位阶 (tier)】 4 contaminated 【领域卡 (domain card)】 later when you 【长臂 (reach)】 the appropriate level.

When you gain access to a contaminated 【领域卡 (domain card)】, you might gain the 【卡牌 (card)】 immediately upon purging contamination, you might gain it when you next level up, or you might gain it in a dramatic situation when you need to push yourself to the limit.

# **Learning from NPCs**

Characters who have survived extended exposure to Delerium manifest incredible powers, and some of those NPCs can be convinced to share what they have learned with PCs.

Here are some NPCs you might consider for teachers that can pass on 【知识 (knowledge)】 of contaminated 【领域 (domain)】 cards, arranged by 【领域 (domain)】. You might also consider creating specific NPCs associated with factions that the PCs have a good relationship with, especially if other options for the relevant domains don't make sense for your party or your version of Drakkenheim.

- **【利刃 (Blade)】, 【骸骨 (Bone)】, 【勇气 (Valor)】.** The Ironhelm siblings, Ansom and Petra Lang
- **【贤者 (Sage)】, 【午夜 (Midnight)】.** Old Zoya, Eoghan Ghostweaver
- **【奥术 (Arcana)】, 【典籍 (Codex)】.** Oscar Yoren, the Pale Man
- **【优雅 (Grace)】, 【辉耀 (Splendor)】.** Ryan Greymire, the Duchess, the Queen of Thieves

An 【非玩家角色 (NPC)】 may ask for a favor or task from the 【玩家角色 (PC)】 before agreeing to teach them, and learning contaminated 【领域 (domain)】 cards from NPCs should probably take the form of a Long-Term Project. The higher the level of the contaminated 【卡牌 (card)】, the longer the project. Additionally, learning these powerful abilities may be dangerous on its own or require the 【玩家角色 (PC)】 to face dangers in order to access the greater power. Manuals, Spellbooks, and other Artifacts

Perhaps the powerful variations on 【领域 (domain)】 abilities existed before the meteor, or maybe these techniques can be learned from a text and not just from a living teacher inperson. Characters might stumble upon contaminated spell cards or 【术典 (grimoires)】 in a NPCs' spellbook, like that of Oscar Yoren or Ryan Greymire. Perhaps a 【战士 (warrior)】 from a past delved into the city survived long enough to write down the techniques they developed and their manual survives long after they succumbed to the city's dangers.

# **Rat's Nest Tavern**

*"My colleagues have an experiment in mind that requires a large delirium crystal. Smaller shards won't suffice. Unfortunately, such specimens are quite rare in the Outer City ruins. However, we've got a lead…"*

A small chunk of the meteor broke off and crashed into the Rat's Nest Tavern in the Sprawl, utterly destroying most of the building. However, an intact delerium crystal was left behind. Unfortunately, a ratling colony has created a burrow under the taphouse basement and moved the meteorite into their warrens. The ratlings have grown bold enough to launch raids throughout the ruins and take several captives in the process.

# **Overview**

In this adventure, the characters travel to the Rat's Nest Tavern to obtain a delerium crystal or rescue a captive prisoner. How the characters accomplish either is up to them: they can fight, sneak past, or trick the ratlings, but the creatures will viciously defend their lair by responding with ambushes and traps of their own.

## **Adventure Hooks**

**Good Prospects.** (Amethyst Academy) River contacts the characters for a clandestine meeting. She wants them to retrieve a delerium crystal from the Rat's Nest.

A crystal of this 【体型 (size)】 is normally worth about 2 sacks of gold, but she offers 2 sacks and a 【袋 (bag)】.

- River's intel is based on rumours the tavern was 【被摧毁 (destroyed)】 by a breakaway piece of the original meteor. Since her intel is based on speculation, she offers the characters a *【轻度伤害 (minor)】 potion of healing* each as an 【推进 (advance)】 in the event that the characters 【轮次 (turn)】 up nothing.
- River cannot disclose the nature of the experiment*:*

"Your continued discretion ensures continued employment."

*Missing in 【动作 (Action)】.* (Hooded Lanterns) Petra Lang approaches the characters in Emberwood Village. Her brother, Ansom Lang, was captured when ratlings 【遇袭 Ambushed (ambushed)】 them during a supply run last night. She needs the characters to rescue Ansom before it's too late. She offers 5 bags of gold as a reward for his safe return.

- 【对抗 (Against)】 orders to retreat, Petra tracked the ratlings to the Rat's Nest. She was driven off when the ratlings wounded her with a contaminated sling bullet. Petra explains that ratlings often keep prisoners to have a "fresh meal" later. She's positive Ansom is still alive.
- Petra warns the characters about the dangers of contamination. She can't help the characters personally as she's recovering from contamination herself. She advises them that if they get contaminated in the ruins, Flamekeeper Hanna in Emberwood Village can help them with her magic.
- Finally, she promises to put in a good word with the Lord 【指挥官 (Commander)】 for the characters' help. However, she emphasizes this is a personal job - she's already risking insubordination:

*"Amongst the Hooded Lanterns, our survival doctrine says we don't risk lives on 'futile' rescue attempts. We all knew the dangers when we signed up, but he's my brother."*

![](_page_17_Picture_1.jpeg)

For the most part, the ratlings in the burrow are hostile to trespassers of any sort. They know who lives in the burrow and who does not, and can recognize each other by scent. They lure intruders into traps or ambushes where the ratlings can use their numbers to their advantage. The ratlings are most active at night, when two-thirds leave the burrow on foraging raids. During the daylight hours, most ratlings are sleeping in their dens except for a few sentries.

## **The Rat Prince**

*Bully, only logical when it comes to food, repeats himself in conversation*

【地下之民 (Underborne)】 Ratling (he/him)

A large and ferocious ratling who self-styles himself as the Rat Prince (see Appendix A). He rules this burrow because he's the biggest, strongest, and killed the last ratling who called themselves the Rat Prince.

**Roleplaying:** When speaking, he frequently repeats words three times for emphasis, "Yes yes yes". He believes once his colony has enough food, they will be able to grow large and multiply. Then all the other ratlings will bow down to him for sure!

**【希望点 (Hope)】:** Gather enough food so he and his colony can grow until he rules all the ratlings!

**【恐惧点 (Fear)】:** Losing power to another bigger, stronger ratling.

*Treasure Hunter, what signs of having killed other adventurers and faction agents does the Rat Prince carry with him?*

## **Squeaks the Seer**

#### *Braggart for the Rat God, cunning, deluded by delerium*

【地下之民 (Underborne)】 Ratling (she/her)

*Description:* This albino ratling warlock of the rat god is the spiritual 【头领 (leader)】 of the colony. She remains popular because she leads cannibalistic rituals in the name of the Rat God.

*Roleplaying:* Squeaks thinks she can talk to the Rat God through the delerium crystal, which she keeps in her lair. She loves to spin tall tales of the Rat God's power and cunning.

**【希望点 (Hope)】:** Pleasing the Rat God by collecting the shiny (delerium).

**【恐惧点 (Fear)】:** Losing the favor of the Rat God by letting the delerium stone be taken.**Missionary**, how can you tell that Squeaks is a false prophet and lying about her insights?

## **Befriending the Ratlings**

In the original *Dungeons of Drakkenheim* 【战役 (campaign)】, the Rat Prince surrendered to the player characters. Using deception to convince the Rat Prince they were ghosts dispatched by the Rat God, the Rat Prince befriended the characters and became a recurring 【非玩家角色 (NPC)】. The player characters continued to protect and hide the ratlings, but once the garmyr were driven out of the city, nothing was holding back a growing ratling population. Left completely unchecked, their numbers swelled to gargantuan proportions that threatened to overtake the entire city!

# **Area Details**

## **Scrag Lane**

The Rat's Nest Tavern lies on the mud-slick and rubble-strewn Scrag Lane south of 【牧者 (Shepherd)】's Way, nestled between ramshackle tenements and crumbling flats. The original tavern has been almost entirely obliterated, and the neighbouring buildings are heavily damaged. What remains are stone foundations piled with debris. The first-floor walls stand about 【箱 (chest)】 high, a few supporting timbers splayed outward like a burst ribcage. The rooftop and upper levels have entirely 【崩倒 (collapsed)】 into a mist-covered crater filled with rubble. The original tavern sign juts out obliquely from a building on the opposite side of the street. It is engraved with a smiling, obese rat laying on a plate of food.

*Survivor, what stories have you heard about the Rat's Nest Tavern? Do you have memories of this place from before the meteor?*

On a successful search of the environs, PCs can discover an entrance to the Drakkenheim sewers, slick with delerium sludge.

# **Developments**

Characters who successfully explore the Rat's Nest might trigger the following developments:

## **The Delerium Crystal**

River upholds her end of the bargain faithfully. If the characters were swift and professional in their dealings with her, she gives them a single dose of aqua delerium as an additional reward. She encourages them to take a few days off to recuperate, promising to contact them shortly (in 1d6 days) with another job. On the other hand, River is much less impressed if the characters fail to recover the delerium crystal. She still offers to buy any found delerium from the characters, but decides to wait and reassess the characters' capabilities before offering them further work.

Characters who aren't already in contact with the Amethyst Academy still have a valuable find on their hands, which could put them in contact with any faction. The Hooded Lanterns know the delerium crystal is worth a small fortune, but they're happy to let the characters keep it as an extra reward for their efforts.

Ansom or Petra could suggest the characters sell the crystal in Emberwood Village to Fairweather Trades and Exports. Another 【非玩家角色 (NPC)】 might suggest the characters ask around the village for contacts with the Amethyst Academy or the Queen's Men to sell the crystal to them. Alternatively, the characters might encounter a knight of the Silver Order, who encourages the characters to destroy the delerium. Finally, a pilgrim of the Falling Fire may claim the crystal has a holy purpose, and try to convince the characters to bring it to Lucretia Mathias at Saint Selina's Monastery as an offering.

## **Rescuing Ansom Lang**

If Ansom Lang survives, he invites the characters to return with him to 【牧者 (Shepherd)】's Gate. His comrades are thrilled at his safe return, and Lord 【指挥官 (Commander)】 Elias Drexel takes note of the characters. He won't meet them personally just yet, but instead makes arrangements for Ansom or Petra to contact them for another mission within 7 (2d6) days.

Petra reminds the characters that their arrangement was a personal matter, and technically not official Hooded Lanterns business. As such, this mission alone won't earn the characters free passage through 【牧者 (Shepherd)】's Gate. However, should the characters come to the gate in an emergency, the Hooded Lanterns will help them. In such an event, they'll overlook the tariffs "just this one time" and let them through.

If the characters fail to rescue Ansom, Petra believes them to be utterly incompetent. She hesitates to involve them further in Hooded Lanterns affairs, and bitterly mistrusts their capabilities in any future situations.

If Ansom dies, but the characters weren't yet working with the Hooded Lanterns, it's up to them whether or not they reveal her fate. Regardless, his comrades are shaken by his disappearance and death. In grief, the hard-hearted Lord 【指挥官 (Commander)】 never speaks of him again. Petra presses on, but it feels like she's missing a part of himself.
